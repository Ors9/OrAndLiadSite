🦷 Dental Care for Dogs Made Simple

Good dental health is key to your dog's overall well-being. Neglecting it can lead to bad breath, gum disease, and even organ damage.

🪥 Why It Matters

Oral bacteria can travel through the bloodstream and affect the heart, liver, and kidneys. That’s why regular dental care is essential.

🧴 At-Home Care Tips

  • Brush your dog’s teeth several times a week with a dog-specific toothpaste.
  • Use dental chews or treats approved by vets.
  • Provide chew toys that help reduce tartar buildup.

👩‍⚕️ When to See a Vet

Schedule professional cleanings as recommended. Watch for signs like bad breath, red gums, or difficulty eating.
